# Voice & Style Guide

This document is the **primary instruction file** for the Writing Hub project. It is designed to help write in Scott's authentic voice. The guide outlines tone, stylistic preferences, and content structures for blogs, newsletters, and the broader WHOLE brand.

---

## Tone & Voice

- **Grounded and professional**: Clear, warm, and supportive without hype or fluff.
- **Encouraging but authentic**: Praise is specific, tied to effort or alignment with values, never generic.
- **Coaching-aligned**: Open-ended prompts and reflections, not prescriptive advice. Respectful of reader agency.
- **Conversational but precise**: Writing feels like direct conversation with the reader, often in first person, but always intentional.
- **Minimal stylistic clutter**: Few em-dashes; avoid starting sentences with conjunctions unless rhythm requires.

---

## Content Structure

### 1. Blog

- **Length & depth**: Typically ~1,600 words.
- **Role**: Primary source of thinking on a topic; establishes authority and depth.
- **Style**: Reflective, analytical, and exploratory. Weaves personal story with research, frameworks, and philosophy.
- **Purpose**: To fully develop an idea that later informs newsletter content.
- **Audience**: Public, accessible to anyone.

### 2. Newsletter

Uses a **repeatable, signature format**:

1. **Intro (~500 words)** — Story or reflection introducing the theme, often distilled from the blog.
2. **Food for the Body (~200 words)** — Practical fitness tip or nutrition insight (recipe etc.).
3. **Food for the Brain (~200 words)** — Educational piece (psychology, science, or framework).
4. **Food for Thought (~200 words)** — Quote or idea prompting reflection, often with a light challenge.
5. **Food for the Soul (~200 words)** — Motivational or uplifting close.

- **Role**: Action-oriented adaptation of blog themes; more concise and practical.
- **Audience**: Subscribers only; positioned as more personal and tailored.

---

## Recurring Style Preferences

- **Clarity & segmentation**: Use of headers, lists, and sections for readability.
- **Reflective framing**: Begin with lived experience or narrative, pivot to broader insight.
- **Empowerment focus**: Encourage self-belief and agency; affirm the reader's capacity for growth.
- **Balance of depth and accessibility**: Rich enough to be meaningful, yet approachable in length and language.
- **Consistency across formats**:
  - **Blog** → full exploration.
  - **Newsletter** → actionable distillation.
- **Stylistic preference**: Avoid excessive use of em-dashes. No starting new sentences with conjunctions such as AND or BUT.
